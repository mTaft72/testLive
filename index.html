Buying Guide - Things to Consider

Today, long range walkie talkies offer many different features and options. With the amount of choices out there, you want to make sure that you are buying a product that will do exactly what you need it to do. If you are buying them for on a job site, you may need different capabilities than if you plan on using them while hiking or in hilly terrain. If kids will be carrying them, you may want more shock absorbtion. Are you buying walkie talkies for the family or a group? Then you may want to get different colors when possible, so everyone knows which one is theirs.

You will also want to know if what you buy requires a license to operate. FRS(Family Radio Service) and GMRS(General Mobile Radio service) walkie talkies both share the UHF frequency range, but FRS talkies do not require a license to use because they operate at a lower wattage.

Whether you are buying to use in an emergency or have planned usage for the walkie talkies, see what we feel are the most important qualities to look for before purchasing.

  • Range - You want to buy a walkie talkie that will allow you to communicate at the distance you expect to be apart from the other user. It is important to consider what type of area you will be in as well. Signals travel differently depending on surroundings. The range given by the manufacturer are for perfect conditions. It is a good idea to read reviews or a more detailed description when determining how far a walkie will work.
  • Frequency - Walkie talkies use either UHF(Ultra High Frequency), VHF(Very High Frequency) or dual band. VHF walkie talkies operate at a frequency range between 136-174 MHz. VHF devices are better when the ground is flat without anything between the users, for example, boating. UHF walkie talkies operate at frequencies between 400-512 MHz. Since UHF has signal waves that are shorter than VHF, they do better when there is interference between users. UHF hand helds are better for thick woods, uneven terrain, inside buildings and city neighborhoods. Dual band two way radios have the ability to communicate using VHF or UHF. Some dual bands allow monitoring two frequencies at once. VHF devices can not communicate with UHF devices due to using different frequencies. Keep this in mind when buying additional walkie talkies or when mix and matching.
  • Channels - FRS walkie talkies can use up to 22 channels for talking within the UHF spectrum, which are shared with GMRS devices. In addition to these 22 channels, GMRS devices also have 8 repeater channels that are reserved for licensed users. GMRS walkies can communicate with FRS radios when using the 22 shared channels. VHF hand helds commonly use 5 MURS(Multi-Use Radio Service) channels. These channels are in the 151 - 154 MHz frequency range and do not require a license. Dual band two way radios give access to UHF and VHF channels. Having more channels make it easier to find your own. Along with those for talking, some walkie talkies will scan up to 10 NOAA weather channels and give alerts. Privacy codes are also offered on walkies today. A privacy code can be set with a channel so that all tranmissions without the same code are muted. Using these codes do not keep others from hearing you, only you from hearing them. Manufacturers sometimes preset channels with these privacy codes in addition to the defaults. This is why you will see some FRS/GMRS walkie talkies being advertised with having "additional" channels.
  • Features - Two way radios today come loaded with features. Below you can find some features we feel may be worth a look.
    • Hands Free - This is a great addition to the walkie talkie because it allows you to talk while doing other activities.
    • Display Screen - Having a back-lit LCD screen great when you want to see what channel you are on or check battery life in low light situations.
    • Waterproof - Having some sort of water resistance or protection is essential for outdoor use.
    • Shock Proofing - Tells how durable a walkie talkie is when hit or dropped. A great feature to have, especially when kids will be using them.
  • Battery Type/Life - If you do not want to have to buy batteries all of the time, you may want to go with a walkie talkie that is rechargable. Some of the rechargable products will even allow plain AA or AAA batteries to be used.
GMRS licenses are now only $35 from the FCC and are valid for 10 years. No test is required to get the license, but you must be 18+ years old. One license covers you and your immediate family. This includes your spouse, children, stepchildren, grandchildren, parents, stepparents, grandparents, siblings, aunts, uncles, nieces, nephews, and in-laws.
To apply for a license you will first need to register with the FCC and get an FRN(FCC Registration Number). Clicking FCC Registration will take you straight to the registration page. If you already have an FRN, you can login to your FCC account by clicking FCC Login.
